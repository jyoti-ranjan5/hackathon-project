Alerts-


Medical Emergency:

An alert could indicate a medical emergency such as a cardiac arrest, severe allergic reaction, or other life-threatening situations. In this case, medical staff would mobilize to provide immediate assistance and care to the patient..

Security Concerns

An alert might indicate a security concern such as a threat, unauthorized individual on the premises, or other safety issues. Hospital security would likely be called upon to address the situation and ensure the safety of patients, staff, and visitors.

Fire or Environmental Hazards:

An alert could be triggered by a fire alarm or detection of environmental hazards such as gas leaks or chemical spills. Evacuation procedures would be followed to ensure everyone's safety.

Equipment Malfunction:

An alert might indicate a malfunction with critical medical equipment, such as ventilators or dialysis machines. Maintenance staff would be alerted to address the issue and ensure equipment is functioning properly.

Natural Disaster:

In the event of a natural disaster like an earthquake, hurricane, or severe weather, hospitals may be put on alert to prepare for potential influx of patients or to ensure readiness for any impacts on infrastructure.
